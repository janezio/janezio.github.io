Fun Things To Do

For those of us who like to go outside as often as possible in the summer, winter can mean prolonged periods of time spent indoors. Here’s a list of my all-time favorite physical activities that you can do indoors and out.

Number one, of course, is skiing at Mount Brighton. Not only is it scenic, you can also learn how to ski there - and I know it can be frightening for beginners! You don’t even have to own a pair of skis to go - you can rent skis and all accompanying equipment on-site.

Secondly, dancing, dancing, dancing. But this is the Midwest, you say. People don’t dance here, you say. Well, that’s just not true…. I dance! Not well. But I do. Anyway. I really recommend Swing Ann Arbor because they have a lesson before every dance. It’s a low-key environment and the instructors are friendly and upbeat. They also play a range of music, which I appreciate.

Third, treat yourself to a show at the Ark, a well-know music venue. I took advantage of Take A Chance Tuesday, which is always free, to see my friend Lizzy Shell. Even though I sat closer to the back than the front, I could hear her voice crystal-clearly. The Ark often hosts bigger names, too, like Joan Baez and Mary Chapin Carpenter.

Lastly, there’s always the Arb. Also known as Nichols Arboretum, the Arb is perhaps Ann Arbor’s most-loved park. It’s got everything: the Huron River, a prairie, and a wide meadow surrounded by hills. It’s easily accessible from Central Campus, though be advised that parking is a nightmare.
